Michael Dunn is an undergraduate student at the University of California Santa Barbara studying Art, Art History, and M.A.T.(math, art, and technology). He primarily works with 2D mediums such as graphite, acrylic paints, and ink. He also works with digital mediums such as Adobe Illustrator, Blender, and Fusion 360. His recent work explores 3D spaces using a combination of digital programs and physical materials. His praxis explores the limitations of the mediums he uses and phenomenological themes. His art also examines themes of change, such as the effects of climate change and technological advancements. Michael soon plans to continue his education at the graduate level.
